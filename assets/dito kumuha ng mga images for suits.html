    Suits Style Advice

    A man must know his specific body type before he can wear a suit jacket that will fit him properly and create the best image possible. There is a suit style to match and enhance each body type.

    Suit for Tall Men

    Tall men are served best in three buttons, single-breasted jackets which minimize their abnormal height.

    Suit for Short Men

    Short men will look best in jackets that maximize their height. This is accomplished best in a one or two button jacket that creates a longer "V". Peak lapels are a great option for shorter men and should be standard on a double-breasted jacket. Avoid three buttons, ventless jackets.

    Suit for Round Men

    Wear one or two button jackets and keep it buttoned unless sitting. A six button double-breasted jacket will balance a hefty man's weight while creating a longer silhouette from shoulder to the waist.

    Shoulders

    The suit's shoulders should hug yours; shoulder pads should not protrude beyond your own shoulders. If you stand sideways against a wall and the shoulder pad touches the wall before your arm does, the suit is too big.

    Chest

    You should be able to easily button the jacket without it straining. Conversely, there shouldn't be too much space between the button and your chest-no more than a fist's worth.

    Length

    When your arms are hanging straight down, you should be able to cup your fingers under the sides of your suit jacket. However, these days, with shorter suits in style, some jackets reach only about an inch beyond the cuff of your suit sleeve.

    Three Buttons

    The three-button suit became the dominant look in the 1990s; it now seems to be the standard young man's choice. Instead of opting for one of those high-cut versions, look for one with a roll-over lapel-one in which you button the middle button, encouraging the soft lapel to roll over the top button.

    Two Buttons

    For years the two-button suit was the go-to conservative, Capitol Hill getup. Now every fashion label imaginable is designing two-button suits, except they're making them more streamlined and modern. This cut is what's most in style right now.

    One Button

    If you're looking for something rakish, a bit more high-style, try a one-button suit. It's not for everyone, but if you can pull it off, it's a sleek look.

    Vents

    A center vent is all-purpose, it is both modern and traditional, You can't go wrong.
    Side vents are more European, a bit more suave.
    A ventless jacket is just plain wrong. It says you think it's still 1986.

    Lapel

    A notch lapel-what you see on most business suits-is the standard. You're always safe with a notch lapel.
    A peak lapel is more old-school and elegant. And now it's enjoying a comeback with the high-fashion crowd. It makes a statement.

    Suit Fabric

    Suit fabric indeed does make the suit figuratively and literally. Wool, cotton, linen, silk, polyester, Cashmere, dacron, viscose, spandex, flannel and tweed are the materials that usually consist of a suit.

    Selecting the right suit lies in more than just finding the right suit style. You have to choose one in a suitable fabric. Because of different seasons, the appropriate suit fabric will differ (although, there are annual staples, like wool, that will be compatible of your style needs all year long).

    Following you will find a brief introduction of the different types of suit material, which's all you need to know about men's suits so that you can pick out the perfect one for yourself.

    WOOL

    Wool suits are normally the most popular suits in the market. It's the most versatile fabric, the best in comfort, wrinkle-resistant, and has greater longevity than others. Its ability to absorb moisture is also an another important characterisitic that makes wool an ideal suit fabric. Thus even when you're forced to sport a suit on the hottest of days, it'll keep you cool. This material can be spun at super 90's all the way up to super 200's. The higher the number the higher the quality.

    COTTON

    Although the cotton suit isn't the most popular suit on the market, it is the most popular fabric for clothing and a durable fabric found in most suits for cotton is cheap, wrinkles easy and is not fated to all types of suits. This material is nice and breathable and makes for a nice comfortable fit. Also the material is inexpensive and therefore these garments are usually much less expensive than their counterparts.

    LINEN

    Wearing a mens linen suit is gonna be one of your more comfortable options when choosing a suit material. Linen is one of the oldest materials in the world and has many advantages as well as disadvantages. These suits are light and resist moisture which makes them suitable for summer or heavy sweaters. Linen may look cool while on a tropical vacation, but it has many shortcomings that don't necessarily suitable for the modern man's daily life.

    SILK

    Silk suits derive from the same insects that ruin suits moths. However they are also some of the most comfortable garments out on the market for them extremely slick and cool to touch. Shimmering material looks so great under club lighting.

    POLYESTER

    Polyester suits is the most fashionable ones, because of its shrink-resistance, easy to maintain and it's color does not fade even after repeated washings. Polyester fabrics can provide specific advantages over natural ones, such as improved wrinkle resistance, durability and high color retention. As a result, polyester fibres are sometimes spun together with natural fibres to produce a cloth with blended properties. Synthetic fibres also can create materials with superior water, wind and environmental resistance compared to plant-derived fibres.

    What if my suit does not fit?

    If your order does not fit, Honeybuy will cover alterations of your garments up to $79.99 or remake it throughly from new materials through a remake order.     You can visit any local alterations shop to get your clothing altered. Honeybuy will cover up to $79.99 of your alterations bill. We may be able to recommend an alterations shop in your area as well, please contact us at Service@Honeybuy.com with any questions.

    How long does it take to get a remake?

    Once the updated measurements and request have been sent to us, we will start your remake. You will receive the remade Suit within 21 days upon your submission. Please check your order status in your User Center for updates.

    My recent order does not fit as expected, what should I do?

    Please refer to Honeybuy's alterations guide firstly which will show you whether your suit can be altered at a local tailor. If it can, we will refund you the alteration cost. To claim an alterations credit, you must log in, click on 'My orders', copy the order number and click on 'Message box'. Then compose and leave a message for a credit (up to $79.99).

    If your suit cannot be altered by a local tailor or requires more than $79.99 of alterations, we will remake the suit for you. To apply for a remake, please Login and click on 'My Measurements' to Edit Measurements then click on 'My orders', copy the order number and click on 'Message box'to compose and leave a message for us.We will review your updated Measurements and you will receive your remake within 14 days upon your submission.

    Measurements

    How are the measurements I submit online used to create my Honeybuy custom suit?

    The measurements that you submit via the self-measurement process are not your final garment measurements, but used as a base to guide us in making your perfect fitting suit. At Honeybuy, your suit will go through a 3 step measurement review process to ensure ideal fit:
    1.    You measure yourself following our guide.
    2.    Our team may adjust your measurements based on the cut standards for someone your weight and height.
    3.    A Honeybuy Master Tailor then further refines your measurements based on his/her years of experience hand cutting suits.

    Should I need a Tailor to take my Measurements?

    No. Honeybuy's measuring process was designed so that anyone can measure themselves under the help of a friend in 10 minutes. We find the best success not comes from using a tailor because our measurement system is unique and created for easy at-home use.

    Can I base my measurements on a well fitting suit that I own?

    Our measuring methods are unique to us and differ from the measurements used for other clothing brands. All Honeybuy orders are custom tailored to match your physical measurement profile, and for this reason we are unable to accept measurements from any garments that you own from another manufacturer.

    Can I use measurements taken by my tailor?

    We recommend you use our easy-to-follow 'Measured Process'. Our measuring methods are unique to us and differ from the measuring methods used at a traditional tailor. If a tailor measures you the way that fits his tailoring style, it may cause discrepancies and ill-fitting garments. If you still want to visit a tailor to be measured, please download this Tailor's Form to limit any issues.

    I want to change measurements on my current order, is this possible?

    Depending on the stage of your order, Honeybuy can adjust as desired. If your order status in your Account is not 'Paid' you can update your profile online and it will be used in the current order. All orders are given a 24 hour grace period to make changes to the measurements and customizations.

    I am not skinny like the models in the photos, will Honeybuy suits and apparel still fit me?

    Honeybuy suits are 100% custom made specifically according to your measurement profile. No matter what your size or body type is, our suits are built to flatter. Once receiving your order, we encourage you to take good advantage of our industry leading Perfect Fit Promise to ensure your suit fits exactly the way you want.

    Can your suits be customerizing made for big guys or even for children?

    Honeybuy customers come in all shapes, sizes, and ages. All our products are hand tailored according to your submitted measurements, so there are no limitations in regarding to large or small measurements.
    Once ordering for a child, please email us your order number and tell us know the suit is for a child so that we can pass this information along to our tailor.

    Can I manage multiple measurements from one account?

    Usaully a Honeybuy client can only have one set of measurements for each garment type (i.e. 1 suit measurement, 1 shirt measurement, etc.) in one profile. If you wish to have multiple suit profiles, you will need to create a second account with another email address.
